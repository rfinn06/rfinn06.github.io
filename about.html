About Me

Personal Background

I grew up in the south suburbs of Chicago and feel that I am a midwesterner through and through. In my mind, being a midwesterner means that you are welcoming, hardworking, and always willing to lend a helping hand. These are values that are important to me and I try to uphold them to the best of my ability. I am proud to be from the midwest and feel that being so has made me the person I am today.

Educational Background

Growing up I was lucky enough to receive a Catholic education. There are two reasons why I am thankful for the education I recieved. The first is that the experience provided me with a strong sense of self. I learned what was important to me and what my values are. The second reason I am thankful for my Catholic education is that it instilled in me the importance of community.

After high school I attended the University of Wisconsin-Madison. This was the first time in my life I was going to be in a place where I didn't know anyone. This was a great experience. It was almost like having to re-introduce myself to the world. I received a great education both in and outside of the classroom.

Interests and Hobbies

One of my hobbies includes collecting vinyl records. There are two things I love about listening to vinyl that you don't get from digital music. The first thing I love is the sound of vinyl. There is a texture to the sound that fills a room when you're listening to a record. The other thing I love about vinyl is that it is a completely different listening experience than listening to music digitally. When you are listening to music digitally, you tend to jump around a lot between songs and artists. When you are doing this you are passively listening to the music.

Listening to a vinyl record is a more immersive experience. I liken it to spending time with a good book. The relationship between yourself and the music becomes more personal and thought provoking. I encourage you to sit down and listen to a record.
